## 1) What have I been doing this week? What skills did I need?

In Week 5, the focus remained on the membership homepage redesign of the European Heat Pump Association, but instead of moving directly into implementation, we shifted our attention to refining the content and preparing all necessary elements before building the page in WordPress.

Following the latest feedback, we realized that some aspects of the structure and messaging still needed adjustment. As a result, we took a step back to rethink certain parts of the page and ensure that both the structure and the content were fully aligned before implementation.

A large part of this week was therefore dedicated to copywriting and content planning. We worked on defining the exact text that would appear on the homepage, ensuring that the messaging was clear, engaging, and aligned with the goal of encouraging users to become members.

This included:

- Refining the value proposition text
- Rewriting and simplifying sections to improve clarity
- Structuring content so it is easy to scan
- Ensuring consistency with the organization's tone and branding

In addition to copywriting, we also focused on selecting the visual assets that will be used on the page. This involved reviewing available images and choosing visuals that support the message, make the page more engaging, and align with the organization's identity.

Throughout the week, we continued refining the page by:

- Adjusting sections based on the latest feedback
- Improving how content and structure work together
- Ensuring that the page clearly communicates benefits and guides users toward membership

I actively contributed by participating in content discussions, suggesting improvements to the wording of certain sections, and helping refine how the information is presented. I also contributed to organizing content and ensuring that the structure supports readability and conversion.

This week required:

- Copywriting and UX writing
- Content structuring and prioritization
- Attention to detail in messaging
- Collaboration and discussion
- Conversion-focused thinking

The main objective was to ensure that both content and visuals were fully prepared before starting implementation, so that the next phase can proceed more efficiently.

## 3) What did I learn this week? In what way?

This week helped me understand that strong content is just as important as structure in UX design.

Even with a well-designed layout, unclear or weak messaging can reduce the effectiveness of a page.

I learned that:

- Copywriting plays a key role in conversion
- Content must be clear, concise, and easy to understand
- Visuals should support the message, not just decorate the page
- Refining content before implementation makes the development process more efficient
