## 1) What have I been doing this week? What skills did I need?

In Week 8, I continued working on the implementation of the membership homepage in WordPress, with a strong focus on refining existing sections and resolving responsiveness issues encountered over the past weeks.

While the previous weeks were more focused on building and implementing sections, this week shifted more toward restructuring and improving the existing layout to ensure proper behavior across all screen sizes.

Over the past three weeks, I had already been introduced to responsiveness and had received guidance on how to approach it. In Week 6, I encountered initial issues and received clear explanations from my colleagues, which helped me resolve specific problems and continue implementing the rest of the page.

However, as I continued working, I became increasingly frustrated with responsiveness when elements did not behave as expected. Instead of consistently applying responsive logic, I decided to first implement the full page and planned to adjust responsiveness afterward.

In practice, this approach led to significant rework. Because many sections were not built with responsiveness in mind, I had to go back and restructure multiple components, which resulted in duplicated effort and increased development time.

A clear example of this was the hero/header section. Initially, I implemented it using a grid layout to create two separate content areas on the left and right. While this worked visually on desktop, the layout did not adapt correctly on tablet and mobile devices. The grid structure was too rigid, and the content did not reflow properly across breakpoints.

As a result, I had to rebuild the entire hero section from scratch using a flexbox-based structure. This allowed for more flexibility in alignment, spacing, and stacking behavior across different screen sizes.

Throughout this process, my colleagues were very supportive and helpful. They took the time to explain and demonstrate why it is crucial to consider responsiveness from the very beginning of development. Their guidance helped me better understand how layout decisions directly affect behavior across devices.

This week, I focused on:

- Restructuring sections to ensure proper responsiveness  
- Replacing rigid grid layouts with more flexible flexbox structures  
- Adjusting spacing, alignment, and stacking behavior  
- Testing layouts across desktop, tablet, and mobile  
- Improving consistency and stability across the page  

In addition, I began creating my own elements using HTML and CSS, which allowed for more precise control over structure and styling beyond the default WordPress blocks.

By this third week of working in WordPress, I now feel very comfortable using the environment. Navigating the editor, structuring layouts, and troubleshooting issues has become significantly more efficient.

This week required:

- Strong understanding of responsive design  
- Practical application of flexbox and grid  
- Problem-solving and debugging  
- HTML and CSS implementation  
- Attention to detail across breakpoints

## 3) What did I learn this week? In what way?

This week was a major learning milestone in my implementation process.

I learned that:

- Responsiveness must be considered from the beginning, not added later  
- Incorrect layout structure leads to significant rework  
- Flexbox is essential for building flexible and responsive layouts  
- Understanding layout systems reduces frustration and trial-and-error  
- Building custom elements allows greater control within WordPress  

Looking back, even though I believed during the previous two weeks that I had already mastered responsiveness, this week showed me that my understanding was still incomplete.

It was only through rebuilding and restructuring multiple sections that I truly understood how crucial it is to plan layouts correctly from the start.

Due to the challenges I faced earlier in the week with responsiveness, flexbox, and grid, I gained a much deeper and more practical understanding of how these systems function. By the end of the week, I became significantly more comfortable working with them and developed a completely different approach to implementation.

I now think not only about how a layout looks, but how it behaves across all screen sizes from the moment I start building.

My colleagues also pointed out that this experience, although challenging, was extremely valuable, as it taught me a lesson I am unlikely to repeat in the future.
