## 1) What have I been doing this week? What skills did I need?

In Week 10, I focused on finalizing the implementation of the membership homepage in WordPress, ensuring that all existing sections are fully functional, interconnected, and consistent across the entire page.

A major part of this week was dedicated to connecting all interactive elements. This included linking buttons, navigation elements, and internal sections so that the page functions as a complete and coherent user experience. Compared to previous weeks, the focus was less on building new components and more on refining, testing, and ensuring everything works as expected.

At this stage, almost the entire membership page is completed and functional. Users are now able to navigate through the page smoothly, and all main sections are aligned both visually and structurally.

The only section that is not yet finalized is the **membership benefits section**. This is because it is directly linked to the ongoing process of digitalizing the benefits content from a static PDF into a more interactive and user-friendly format.

In parallel to the implementation work, we also began actively working on the digitalization of the PDF content. During this week, we held meetings to further define the structure and layout of the future digital version. Together with the team, we discussed how the content should be reorganized, what sections should be prioritized, and how the information can be presented in a clearer and more engaging way.

We also created rough sketches outlining where different types of content should be placed and how users would navigate through the information.

## 3) What did I learn this week? In what way?

This week highlighted the importance of final testing and refinement in the development process.

I learned that:

- Interconnecting elements is essential for a complete user experience  
- Small inconsistencies can impact the overall quality of a page  
- Testing across devices is necessary to ensure stability  
- Finalization requires as much attention as initial implementation  
- Structuring content early helps simplify future implementation  

I also realized that finishing a project is not only about building all components, but about ensuring that everything works together seamlessly.
