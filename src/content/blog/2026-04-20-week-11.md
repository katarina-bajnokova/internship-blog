## 1) What have I been doing this week? What skills did I need?

In Week 11, my work took an unexpected turn due to a technical issue.

The draft I had been working on for the membership homepage was not saved correctly, and at the same time, our IT support restarted the servers. As a result, a large part of my implementation work was lost.

This situation forced me to rebuild the page. While this was initially frustrating, it quickly turned into a valuable learning experience.

Because I had already implemented the page once, I understood the structure, layout logic, and technical decisions behind it. This allowed me to recover the work much faster than expected. Tasks that previously took me around two weeks were completed again in approximately three days.

The reimplementation process felt significantly more efficient. I made fewer mistakes, worked more confidently, and avoided much of the earlier trial-and-error. While rebuilding, I also introduced small improvements and refinements based on what I had learned.

After recovering the page, I received final feedback at the end of the week (Friday). These adjustments will be implemented next week and mainly focus on final refinements such as:

- Adjusting responsiveness for larger screens (the header was tested on laptop, tablet, and mobile, but not yet optimized for very wide displays)
- Replacing abbreviations with the full name "European Heat Pump Association"
- Ensuring all buttons follow consistent formatting (starting with capital letters)
- Increasing the size of logos to improve visibility and balance

In addition, it was decided that the map currently visible in the implementation will be replaced. The original approach will be changed to a different type of map layout, which will better align with the overall design and user experience. Updating and implementing this new map version will also be part of next week's work.

In parallel to recovering the main project, I also continued working on a smaller "side task".

Alongside the membership page, I contributed ideas for improving the program structure and design for the Heat Pump Forum 2026.

This involved thinking about:

- Program structure and hierarchy
- Layout clarity and readability
- Improving navigation through the program

I explored how the program could be made more structured, visually clear, and user-friendly, and suggested a new design direction.

## 3) What did I learn this week? In what way?

This week taught me one of the most important practical lessons so far:

Always save work properly and consistently.

I realized that postponing tasks with the mindset of "I will do it later" can lead to unnecessary risks, especially in technical environments where unexpected issues can occur.

In addition, I learned that:

- Experience significantly improves efficiency
- Rebuilding is much faster when the logic is understood
- Mistakes can reinforce learning more effectively than smooth progress
- Technical setbacks are part of real-world development
