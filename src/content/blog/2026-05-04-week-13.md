## 1) What have I been doing this week? What skills did I need?

In Week 13, I continued working on refinements for the membership project while also focusing on a new challenge related to the Heat Pump Forum programme structure.

At the beginning of the week, I proposed a creative solution for how Day 2 of the Forum programme could still be referenced on the main Forum page without presenting it as an officially organized EHPA event.

The challenge came from the fact that Day 2 is not directly organized by the organization itself, meaning it cannot legally be marketed as part of the official Forum programme in the same way as Day 1.

To address this, I worked on a UX and communication solution that would allow visitors to still discover Day 2 content while making the distinction between the events clear and transparent.

Below is the proposal I created for this solution:

<img src="/internship-blog/images/forumDay2Suggesiton.jpeg" alt="Forum Day 2 suggestion proposal" width="80%" />

The proposal focused on:

- Creating a clearer separation between Day 1 and Day 2  
- Maintaining visual consistency with the Forum page  
- Allowing users to still discover related content naturally  
- Avoiding confusion regarding event ownership and organization  
- Presenting Day 2 in a more informative rather than promotional way  

Initially, the proposal was positively received and accepted within my department. The team appreciated the structure and the communication approach behind the solution.

However, later during the week, the proposal was reviewed by the policy team.

After their review, it became clear that legally we were not allowed to implement the solution. The concern was that the European Commission could interpret the design and presentation as the organization trying to market the external event as its own official programme.

Because of this, the idea could not move forward into implementation, despite the department agreeing with the UX direction itself.

Although the solution was ultimately rejected for legal reasons, the experience was still very valuable because it showed me how professional UX decisions are sometimes influenced by factors beyond design and usability alone.

In parallel to the Forum discussions, we also continued working on the digitalization of the membership benefits PDF.

This week focused heavily on ideation, structure, and layout planning for how the benefits should be transformed into a more interactive web experience.

After multiple discussions and evaluations, we agreed on a structure where each membership benefit would be represented as an individual interactive card.

Instead of presenting all benefits in one very long scrolling page, the idea is to create a cleaner and more engaging interface where users can quickly scan the available benefits and click on a card to learn more about a specific one.

The concept works as follows:

- Each benefit will appear as a separate visual card  
- Users can browse all benefits more easily  
- Clicking on a card will open additional information about that specific benefit  
- The structure keeps the page shorter, cleaner, and more interactive  
- The experience becomes easier to navigate and less overwhelming for users  

This approach significantly improves scanability and user engagement compared to the original static PDF format.

Because my internship is now approaching its final weeks, we also had to realistically evaluate the remaining available time.

As a result, we decided that my main implementation responsibility will be creating the reusable card component structure and integrating it into WordPress.

The goal is to successfully implement the visual card system and create all 12 benefit cards within the page structure.

However, due to time limitations, we concluded that there likely will not be enough time left during my internship to fully implement every detailed modal popup or expanded content section for each individual benefit.

Even so, building the reusable card system and establishing the interactive structure creates a strong foundation that can later be expanded upon after my internship.

In parallel to these discussions, I also continued refining smaller parts of the membership page implementation and ensuring that the recent design decisions from the previous weeks remained visually consistent across the interface.

Overall, this week required:

- Creative problem-solving  
- UX communication thinking  
- Information architecture planning  
- Adaptability when facing constraints  
- Collaboration across departments  
- Attention to detail and refinement work

## 3) What did I learn this week? In what way?

This week taught me that UX and communication decisions are not only shaped by user experience and visual design, but also by legal, institutional, technical, and time-related constraints.

I learned that:

- Some UX solutions may be technically and visually strong but still impossible to implement due to external constraints  
- Communication clarity is extremely important in institutional environments  
- Legal and branding considerations can directly impact design decisions  
- Structuring information into interactive components can greatly improve usability  
- Time limitations require prioritizing the most impactful implementation work  
- Even rejected ideas can still represent valuable contributions and learning experiences  

I also learned the importance of designing solutions that balance user experience goals with organizational limitations and available development time.
