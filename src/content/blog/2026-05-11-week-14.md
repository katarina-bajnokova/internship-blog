## 1) What have I been doing this week? What skills did I need?

This week marked the final week of my internship.

The main focus was completing the remaining implementation work related to the digitalization of the membership benefits section and preparing everything for handover before my internship ended.

During the previous weeks, we had already agreed on transforming the original static PDF into a more interactive structure using reusable benefit cards. This week, I finalized the implementation of that structure inside WordPress.

I created and integrated all 12 benefit cards that will later be connected to popup modals or expandable content sections in future development phases.

The idea behind this structure is to avoid overwhelming users with a very long static page and instead create a more interactive experience where users can quickly scan all available benefits and choose which ones they want to explore further.

This week mainly involved:

- Finalizing the reusable card component structure  
- Integrating all 12 cards into the page  
- Refining spacing, responsiveness, and alignment  
- Ensuring visual consistency with the rest of the membership page  
- Preparing the structure so future modal functionality can easily be added later  

Because my internship was approaching its end, we also had to make realistic decisions regarding what could still be implemented within the remaining time. While the modal popups themselves were not fully developed yet, the entire interactive structure and layout foundation are now prepared for future expansion.

Alongside the implementation work, the week also focused on wrapping up the internship itself.

During the last days, I had several conversations and meetings with the team where we reviewed the projects I worked on over the past months and discussed the progress that had been made throughout the internship.

We talked about:

- The redesign and implementation of the membership page  
- The digitalization direction of the benefits PDF  
- My contributions to the Heat Pump Forum project  
- The technical and UX skills I developed  
- The evolution of my role throughout the internship  

Looking back at everything we worked on made me realize how much my role had changed since the beginning.

At the start of the internship, I was mostly observing meetings, learning workflows, and trying to understand how WordPress and UX processes function in a professional environment.

Over time, I became much more involved in discussions, implementation tasks, UX ideation, and technical problem-solving. By the end of the internship, I was independently implementing responsive sections, creating reusable structures, contributing ideas during meetings, and working directly on live projects.

Toward the end of the week, I organized and cleaned up my remaining project files and implementation work so everything could easily be continued after my departure.

On the final day, I returned the company laptop and completed the final administrative tasks related to the internship.

The atmosphere during the final day felt very different compared to the beginning of the internship. After spending around three months working closely with the team, the environment had become very familiar and comfortable. Saying goodbye felt strange because the internship gradually stopped feeling like a temporary school assignment and started feeling like being part of an actual professional team.

Before leaving, I had the opportunity to thank the people I worked with for their guidance, patience, and support throughout the internship. We also talked briefly about the progress I had made since the beginning, especially regarding implementation confidence and technical understanding.

Overall, this week required:

- Final implementation and refinement skills  
- Attention to detail  
- Component-based thinking  
- Collaboration and communication  
- Professional organization and handover preparation

## 3) What did I learn this week? In what way?

This final week reinforced several important lessons about professional work environments and long-term project development.

I learned that:

- Building reusable and scalable structures is important for future development  
- Projects often continue evolving after individual contributors leave  
- Clear handovers and organization make collaboration easier for future work  
- Time limitations require prioritizing the most impactful tasks  
- Reflection helps recognize growth that is not always obvious during day-to-day work  

Looking back at the full internship, I also realized how much my confidence and technical understanding improved over time.

At the beginning, many aspects of WordPress, responsiveness, layout systems, and frontend implementation felt unfamiliar and sometimes overwhelming. Over the course of the internship, those tasks gradually became much more natural and manageable.

I also learned that growth often comes through mistakes, rebuilding, feedback, and repetition rather than through smooth progress alone.
